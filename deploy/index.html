Beautiful and useless

I awaited the delivery of my Smeg toaster like a dog waiting for his master to return home after a long day. I almost found myself barking as the USPS truck rolled up my street. Thankfully I refrained from running towards the mailman with my tongue hanging out of my mouth, but it was difficult. Oh if only he knew the beauty that lay within that box! I'd had my eyes on this extortionately priced bread warmer for years and I'd finally achieved the perfect balance of stupidity and wealth to pay $250 for a toaster. But oh the toasting possibilities!

Let me confirm that this little metal box is less of a toaster and more of a hairdryer for bread. Both my girlfriend and I own one of these each and both little metal boxes perform as incompetently. My freshly made sourdough is insulted as it's ejected from the toaster, sunburned but not browned. "That's it?" I hear it cry. I'm not sure this toaster has ever produced an equally toasted slice for me in the year that I've owned it yet, like a beautiful woman that treats you badly, I've kept putting up with her **** because she looks good sitting on my kitchen counter. Have I gotten rid of her? No. Do I still tolerate her ineptitude? Yes. Am I going to stop complaining and exchange her for another, less attractive toaster? No, probably not.

So you win Smeg. Once again you've created an inferior product that looks much better than all the others, and because I'm a vain, superficial idiot when it comes to kitchen appliances I will continue to eat warm bread while the more intelligent customers out there settle for an uglier appliance and enjoy perfectly crisp toast every morning.

Lucky bastards.
